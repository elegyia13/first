Taylor Swift!

chronicle.

Era 2

Fearless

01

FEARLESS is Taylor's second studio album, but it's the first to be treated as a number one album. It's also the album that earned her her first Grammy. YOU BELONG WITH ME peaked at number two on the Billboard charts. The title of FEARLESS was inspired by Tim McGraw.

Era 3

Speak now

02

SPEAK NOW is an album that Taylor Swift composed and wrote all the songs herself, and although it is a country album, it has stronger pop and rock colours. It is an album that tells the story of different things and events that she has gone through and different relationships, including romantic relationships, through poetic lyrics.

Era 4

Red

03

The RED albums were criticised for being disjointed, with many different genres - pop, rock and country - on one album. Today, RED is Taylor's most critically acclaimed album to date, and the blurred line between country and pop that was controversial at the time is now what makes it 'the most Taylor Swift album ever'.

Era 5

1989

04

1989 was Taylor's first album as a country singer, but she had no problem with the pop genre. In fact, it was the epitome of pop. It started with pop and it ended with pop. The album is at the centre of Taylor Swift's career and is considered one of the best albums of the 2010s, both musically and commercially, winning her a second Grammy Award for Album of the Year.

Era 6

Reputation

05

Reputation is a dark, emotionally restrained album, unrecognisable from the Taylor Swift of old. Prior to the release of the album, she was ridiculed as a 'snake' after a conflict with a couple. In this album, Taylor talks about issues in her life, unlike her previous albums. However, the latter tracks are more mellow, including a love song for her significant other.

Era 7

Lover

06

Lover is a complete contrast to album six. There are traces of the pursuit of easy listening. As the title of the seventh album is 'Lover', love songs make up the majority of the album. The exploration of love runs throughout the album, and in addition to love songs, there are also songs that look at society and make their voices heard.

Era 8

Folklore

07

The Folklore album is a first-hand account of 'past decisions' and 'past relationships' before COVID-19. It is said to be the album that best captures the emotions she experienced during the lockdown. It's not easy for a singer to change genres, and this is Taylor's second attempt. The album won Taylor her third Album of the Year award at the Grammys.

Era 9

Evermore

08

The Evermore album is known as the sister album to the eighth album and is generally regarded as experimental music. It contains many of Taylor's most unusual sounds. Despite being released only five months after Folklore, the album was praised by many media for its high level of sophistication.

Era 10

Midnights

09

The Midnights album tells the story of "thirteen sleepless nights she's had in her life", which naturally lends itself to a nocturnal mood. The album is also an expression of Taylor Swift's entire inner life. With this album, Taylor Swift became the first artist to win the Grammy Award for Album of the Year four times.

Era 11

TTPD

10

More honest and introspective than its predecessor, THE TORTURED POETS DEPARTMENT is built around a darker, more subdued synth sound, and the emotional arc of the album changes drastically throughout. And it's a record-breaking album.
